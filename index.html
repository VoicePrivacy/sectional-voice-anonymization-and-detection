A Preliminary Study on Sectional Voice Anonymization and Evaluation

Facilitated by the speech generation framework that disentangles the speaker attribute from the original speech and represents it with an embedding vector, voice anonymization is accomplished by substituting the original speaker embedding vector with that of a pseudo-speaker. So far, the anonymization techniques are required to anonymize an original utterance to a single pseudo-speaker, while lacking the detection of the presence of multiple pseudo-speakers in the anonymized speech. To this end, this paper investigates a sectional voice anonymization method by partitioning the original utterance into sections and replacing the original speaker with distinct pseudo-speakers in each section. Subsequently, a detection algorithm is proposed to decide the presence of multiple pseudo-speakers in anonymized speech. Experimental results on the LibriSpeech dataset demonstrated that the sectional anonymization method was able to enhance voice privacy protection capability.
