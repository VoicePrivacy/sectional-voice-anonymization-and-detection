An Anonymizer of High Uniqueness and Efficiency in Voice Anonymization

Abstract: Pseudo-speaker construction presents a fundamental challenge in the state-of-the-art voice anonymization technique, which is based on substituting original speaker attributes with those of a pseudo-speaker. However, current pseudo-speaker construction methods are limited in the uniqueness of pseudo-speakers and computational efficiency. To this end, this paper proposes a feedforward network that maps speaker identities to speaker vectors, achieving pseudo-speaker uniqueness by assigning a distinct identity to each pseudo-speaker. Experimental results obtained from the LibriSpeech dataset demonstrated the effectiveness of the proposed method in enhancing the uniqueness of pseudo-speakers, thereby improving the voice privacy protection capability. Furthermore, the feedforward architecture significantly enhances computational efficiency compared to existing model-based pseudo-speaker generation methods.
